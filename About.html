Indian agriculture has been around since 9000 BC. Agriculture has been one among the main aspects of India before her independence in 1947. However, since India gained her independence, the agricultural industrialization or the agriculture industry’s has declined in contributing to the gross domestic product thanks to the increase in industry and repair sectors. The agriculture industry has always provided approximately a third of the India’s gross domestic product and has employed about two-thirds of the population From the Early-1990s. In the 1960s, the revolution had started after realizing the heavy reliance on agricultural imports and trying to not be hooked in to them with a rising population. The revolution started with the introduction of high-yielding seeds, increased use of fertilizers and irrigation. Ironically, even during this point period, the program was started with the assistance of the United States-based Rockefeller Foundation.

Moreover, the govt pays an annual pension of Rs. 6000 to them. This helps them to a minimum of have some earning apart from their profession. Furthermore, the govt provides quotas (reservations) to their children. This ensures that their children get a correct education. All the youngsters should get a correct education in today’s world.
